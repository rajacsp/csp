# The Monk Who Bought 5 BMWs — And Why He Lives at the Top of My Inspiration Repo

I keep a personal repository of people who genuinely move me.
Not motivational quotes. Not LinkedIn hustle culture.
People whose **decisions under pressure** make me rethink my own defaults.

**Suresh Sambandam** sits at the very top of that list.

Here's the full dump — facts, moments, and the stuff that doesn't make it to the Forbes headline.

---

## He Skipped College at 17 to Help His Father

Not because he was rebellious. Because family needed him.
Then a programming class changed everything.
From that point, his journey was driven purely by passion — no one guided him toward software. He found it himself.

No roadmap. No mentor. Just curiosity compounding quietly.

---

## He Got Hired at HP Without a Degree

Let that sit.

Working at HP, he explored rule-based computing — an early form of AI.
He saw firsthand how complex tools locked out business users from software.
That frustration became Kissflow's founding thesis:

> **Make enterprise tech human.**

---

## He Failed. Twice. Then Built Kissflow.

Two products came before Kissflow — and both failed.

- Product 1: An alternative to Microsoft Excel. Didn't stick.
- Product 2: A cloud platform for building business tools online. Didn't scale.
- Product 3: **Kissflow** — launched 2012. Everything changed.

The third time wasn't charm. It was earned through two full pivots and the discipline
to abandon sunk cost and keep moving.

---

## He Sold His Car to Keep the Lights On

This one hits different.

During Kissflow's early financial crisis, Suresh sold his own car — an Innova —
to cover employee salaries. He also ran up credit cards to keep the company alive.

No layoffs. No excuses.
Just a founder who ate the loss himself so the team didn't have to.

---

## He Rewarded Loyalty with BMWs

In 2021, he gifted BMWs to five long-serving Kissflow employees.

The internet called him "The Monk Who Purchased 5 BMWs."
That's a founder who **remembers who showed up when it was ugly.**

Retention isn't a policy at Kissflow. It's a culture backed by real action.

---

## He Co-Founded SaaSBoomi

Asia's largest community-led SaaS founders' movement.
Not to brand himself. To pay it forward.

He spends significant time mentoring first-time entrepreneurs —
the kind of founder who builds pipelines, not pedestals.

---

## He's Building the Next Generation of Tamil Founders

Through **MudhalVC** and its *Idea Pattarai* accelerator —
modeled after Y Combinator — Suresh is nurturing early-stage founders
from Tier 2 and Tier 3 cities in Tamil Nadu.

And through **Dream Tamil Nadu**, he's working toward a trillion-dollar
vision for the state by 2030.

Purpose at scale. Not just product.

---

## Why He's #1 in My Repo

He's not my inspiration because he succeeded.
He's my inspiration because of **how he moved through the moments
when success was not guaranteed.**

The sold car. The third product. The BMWs.
The SaaSBoomi stage. The Tier 3 city founder he's betting on right now.

That's the repo entry. Permanent. No deletion.
